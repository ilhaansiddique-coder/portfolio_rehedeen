## Machine Park

Our embroidery department runs **24 Barudan multi-head machines** in a temperature-controlled environment. Barudan is the industry benchmark for stitch consistency and registration accuracy — critical for small character embroidery on children's garments where poor registration creates safety hazards (sharp thread ends, fraying at stitch entry).

**Technical specifications:**
- Up to **15 thread colours** per design
- Needle count: 15 needles per head
- Minimum stitch area: 5mm × 5mm
- Maximum embroidery area: 400mm × 500mm
- Thread: Madeira Polyneon polyester (colorfast, shrink-stable)

## Embroidery Types

**Flat embroidery** — Standard chest logos, sleeve badges, back neckline text. The most versatile format for kids' apparel; low profile, no irritation risk.

**Puff embroidery** — Foam-backed raised letters and shapes. Common on caps and sweatshirt chest logos. Foam density varies by design weight.

**3D embroidery** — High-profile raised elements. Used on caps, bags, and chest plates where tactile brand differentiation matters.

**Appliqué + embroidery** — Fabric cutouts secured with satin stitch border. Suitable for large design areas where thread density would add excessive weight.

**Patch production** — We produce cut-edge and merrowed-edge patches for sew-on or iron-on application.

## Digitization

Every embroidery design starts with digitization — converting artwork to a stitch file the machine reads. We include digitization in our quoted price. Sample stitch-out is provided within 3 business days of receiving approved artwork.

**What we need from you:**
- Vector artwork (AI, EPS, or PDF) at final size
- Thread colour references (Pantone or Madeira shade card numbers)
- Placement diagram on a garment flat sketch

## Compliance

All threads and backing materials are tested to **OEKO-TEX Standard 100** and comply with EU REACH SVHC restrictions. Thread colourfastness is tested to ISO 105-C06 (washing) and ISO 105-B02 (light). Test reports available per design on request.
