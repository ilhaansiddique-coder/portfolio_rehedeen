## The Brief

NordKids Retail Group approached RaheDeen in January 2025 with a 22-style AW26 brief for their own-brand children's label. The collection spanned boys and girls sizes 2–12y, with styles across jersey tops, jogger sets, hoodies, and lightweight outerwear.

**Key constraints the buyer set:**
- 14-week ex-factory lead time from tech pack approval
- OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification required on all jersey fabrics
- Scandinavian sizing chart (different from standard EU grading)
- Mixed-size ratio packs per carton for direct-to-store delivery
- All hangtags to carry environmental messaging in Swedish and English

## Our Approach

### Production Allocation

We allocated **one dedicated production line** for NordKids across the full 14-week programme. This is not our standard practice for orders under 5,000 pieces, but the collection's complexity (22 styles across two gender categories, three fabric constructions) warranted the isolation.

### Compliance Management

Our compliance team handled all OEKO-TEX certification tracking, coordinating with our certified mill partners in Gazipur to ensure the correct certification class and lot number was logged per fabric roll. For the REACH documentation, we used Intertek's Bangladesh lab for all 22 style test reports.

The buyer's compliance manager requested our documentation 3 weeks before shipment. We delivered a complete compliance pack — test reports, OEKO-TEX certificates, GCC, and packing list — with 5 days to spare.

### Weekly WIP Reporting

Every Friday, NordKids' sourcing team received a production update: style-by-style completion percentage, fabric intake status, and any open queries. There were zero unplanned surprises across the 14-week programme.

## The Results

The collection shipped 6 days ahead of the agreed ex-factory date. NordKids' 3PL received it 4 days before their DC cut-off — enough time to clear customs without an expedite fee.

Pre-shipment inspection was conducted by Bureau Veritas at our Narayanganj facility. **99.8% of garments passed AQL 1.5** on the first pass. The 0.2% failure rate (6 garments in the final inspection lot) was related to a collar stitch tension issue on one hoodie style — identified, reworked, and re-inspected before packing.

No compliance rejections at the buyer's DC. Zero rework on the buyer's end.
