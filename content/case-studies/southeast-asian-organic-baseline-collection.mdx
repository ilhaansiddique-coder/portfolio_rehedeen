## The Challenge

Naturlig Born is a Danish brand specialising in organic baby and toddler clothing. For SS25, they were launching simultaneously into the Thai retail market (through a Bangkok distributor) and their existing Nordic channels. The dual-market launch created a specific challenge: **Thai labelling requirements differ from EU requirements**, and they needed both in a single production run without creating two separate SKUs.

On top of the labelling challenge, the collection had to be GOTS-certified end-to-end — meaning the organic claim needed chain-of-custody documentation from fibre to finished garment.

## Organic Supply Chain Management

GOTS certification is a chain-of-custody standard. Every entity in the supply chain — fibre producer, spinner, knitter, finisher, garment manufacturer — must be individually certified. RaheDeen is GOTS-certified at the garment manufacturing stage. We work exclusively with two GOTS-certified jersey mills in Bangladesh for organic programmes.

For Naturlig Born's SS25 collection, we:

1. Sourced GOTS-certified 180g organic cotton single jersey from our mill partner in Gazipur
2. Coordinated fabric lot number tracking from the mill's GOTS certificate through to our own certificate
3. Maintained transaction certificates for each fabric intake, as required by GOTS 6.0

## Dual-Market Labelling Without Dual SKUs

The Thai labelling requirement (Thai language care instructions + importer declaration) and the EU requirement (EU language care symbols + country of origin) were combined on a **single multi-language label** printed in 5 colours.

- Care instructions: universal ISO symbols (no language dependency)
- EU-specific text: French, German, Swedish, Danish, English on one side
- Thai-specific text: Thai script on the reverse
- Barcode: EAN-13 (EU) and barcode sticker (Thai distributor's system) applied at packing, per destination

This eliminated the need for two separate production runs or relabelling at the distributor's warehouse.

## Formaldehyde-Free Dye Process

Baby and toddler garments destined for EU must test below 75ppm formaldehyde. Naturlig Born requested we target 0 ppm as a brand commitment statement. We used **reactive dyes with formaldehyde-free fixative** across all colourways. Finished garment testing at SGS Dhaka confirmed non-detect (<10ppm detection limit) on all 8 styles.

## Outcome

The collection launched on time in both markets. The 12-week lead time from design brief to buyer's warehouse was achieved without air freight. Naturlig Born's Thailand distributor received a GOTS transaction certificate and a full compliance pack, which they used to register the products with Thai authorities.

The brand now sources three collections per year through RaheDeen. The dual-labelling workflow we established in SS25 is now their standard production brief.
