## What CPSIA Covers

The **Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act (CPSIA)** was signed into US law in 2008 following a series of product recalls involving lead-painted toys and unsafe children's products imported from overseas. For children's apparel, CPSIA imposes mandatory requirements that every US importer must satisfy before products reach consumers.

### Key Requirements for Children's Garments

**Lead content limits:**
- Maximum 100ppm total lead in surface coatings (paints, inks, finishes)
- Maximum 100ppm total lead content in the substrate

**Phthalate limits (for products intended for children under 12):**
- DEHP, DBP, BBP: ≤0.1% by weight
- DINP, DIDP, DnOP: ≤0.1% by weight in accessible parts

**Flammability:**
- Children's sleepwear (sizes 0–14): must comply with **16 CFR 1615/1616** (either inherently flame-resistant fibre, or snug-fitting)

**Tracking labels:**
Every children's product must permanently bear a tracking label containing: manufacturer name, location of manufacture, date of manufacture, and product identifier. RaheDeen prints tracking labels per CPSIA Section 14(a)(5) on every garment — neck label, woven or heat transfer, style-number referenced on the GCC.

## How RaheDeen Manages CPSIA

**1. General Conformity Certificate (GCC):** We issue a GCC for every children's product style we produce for the USA market. The GCC references the applicable CPSC rules, the 3rd-party test reports, and the manufacturer information.

**2. 3rd-party laboratory testing:** We use **SGS** and **Bureau Veritas** — both CPSC-accepted testing labs. Tests cover lead content, phthalates, and flammability. Reports are provided to buyers before shipment.

**3. Documentation package:** Every USA-destined shipment includes the GCC, test reports, and a CPSIA compliance statement per shipment.
