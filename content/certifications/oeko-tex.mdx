## What OEKO-TEX Standard 100 Means

**OEKO-TEX Standard 100** is an independent testing and certification system for textile raw materials and finished products. Every component in a certified product — fabric, thread, buttons, zippers, labels, prints — must be tested and verified as harmless.

The standard tests for **over 100 harmful substances**, including:

- Formaldehyde (free and releasable)
- Heavy metals (lead, cadmium, chromium VI, nickel, arsenic, mercury)
- Pesticide residues (organochlorine and organophosphorus compounds)
- Phthalates
- Azo dyes (that cleave to form carcinogenic amines)
- pH value (skin-compatible range 4.0–7.5)
- Colour fastness

## Product Classes

OEKO-TEX Standard 100 uses four product classes based on skin contact and wearer age:

| Class | Application |
|---|---|
| **Class I** | Baby/toddler articles (up to 36 months) — strictest limits |
| Class II | Articles in direct contact with skin (underwear, bed linen) |
| Class III | Articles not directly touching skin (jackets, outerwear) |
| Class IV | Decoration materials, curtains |

Our infant and toddler garments are certified to **Class I** — the most stringent tier in the standard. Children's garments (3+ years) meet Class II requirements.

## How We Maintain Certification

OEKO-TEX certification requires annual re-testing of all certified fabrics and components. Our mills provide us with valid certificate numbers per fabric lot. When a new lot enters our facility, the certificate is verified against the OEKO-TEX database before the fabric is cut.

Finished garments are spot-tested at our request to verify that the certified fabric's properties are maintained post-production (washing effects, print application, etc.).

## Certificate Validity

Our current facility-level OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification is valid through **31 December 2026**. Buyer-specific product-level certificates are available on request per production lot.
