## What MOQ Actually Means

**Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)** is the smallest number of units a factory will produce of a single style in a single colourway. It is not negotiable in the way price is — it's determined by the economics of the production process.

Understanding why factories set MOQs helps you work with them, not around them.

## Why Factories Set MOQs

**Fabric bulk:** Fabric mills have their own minimums — typically 300–500m per colour per fabric type. A factory can't order 50m of a specific cotton jersey in sky blue without paying for 300m. The MOQ on fabric flows downstream into the garment MOQ.

**Machine setup time:** Every new style requires machine setup — tension adjustment, stitch program loading, guide and presser foot changes. Setup takes 30–60 minutes per machine per style. On a production run of 50 garments, setup cost per unit is prohibitive. On 300 garments, it's acceptable.

**Trim ordering:** Zippers, buttons, snaps, elastic, interlining — each has its own supplier minimum. Aggregating small trim orders adds purchasing complexity and cost.

**For full private label (fabric + trim sourcing included):** Add 500–1,000 pieces to the above minimums for the first run of a new style, to account for sampling costs and fabric over-cut.

## How to Reduce Your Effective MOQ

**1. Consolidate colourways.** If you're ordering 300 pieces of a hoodie but you want 3 colours, each colour runs separately. Order 900 pieces total (300 per colour) — but discuss with your factory whether shared fabric purchasing across colours reduces the per-colour minimum.

**2. Share fabric across styles.** A T-shirt and a long-sleeve top in the same 180g white cotton jersey can share a fabric purchase. Consolidating styles by fabric type lowers the blended MOQ.

**3. Start with a smaller range.** Five styles at 300 pcs each (1,500 total) is more practical for a first run than 15 styles at 100 pcs each. Factories prefer depth over breadth.

**4. Accept standard colourways.** Custom colour development requires dye lab testing and approval. Standard pantone matches from a mill's existing dye program are faster and sometimes available at lower minimums.

## Questions to Ask Before You Sign Off

- Does the MOQ apply per style, per colour, or per size?
- What happens to unused fabric over-cut — is it stored, sold, or charged back?
- Can you reorder a style at the same MOQ as the original run, or is there a different minimum for reorders?
- At what order volume do you get a dedicated production line (faster turnaround)?

## The Honest Reality

MOQs exist because mass production has minimum viable economics. If you're ordering below the stated MOQ, you can negotiate — but expect to pay a premium per unit, or accept a longer lead time while the factory consolidates your order with similar styles from other brands.

The cleanest way to work with MOQs is to plan your range around them from the start, not to try to break them after the fact.
