## What a Tech Pack Is (and Isn't)

A tech pack is a **specification document** — a set of instructions that tells a factory exactly how to build your garment. It is not mood board content, not inspiration imagery, not a design brief. It is a precise, numeric, dimensioned document that a pattern maker, fabric buyer, and production manager can each extract relevant information from independently.

A bad tech pack — incomplete measurements, missing trims, vague stitch instructions — is the #1 cause of sampling delays. The factory will sample to what they have, and what they produce will be wrong.

## The 8 Essential Tech Pack Components

### 1. Construction Drawing (Flat Sketch)
A clean, proportional flat sketch of front and back (and any interior view needed). Vector preferred (AI or EPS). No artistic rendering — the factory reads this as a technical document, not as artwork.

### 2. Bill of Materials (BOM)
Every component in the garment, specified:
- **Fabric:** Composition, weight (GSM), construction (e.g., "160g 95/5 cotton-elastane single jersey"), colour reference (Pantone TCX or mill shade card)
- **Thread:** Composition, colour, supplier reference if branded
- **Interlining:** Type, weight, fusible or sew-in
- **Trims:** Zippers (brand, length, coil size, pull style), buttons (material, size, colour), snaps (material, size, attachment method), elastic (type, width, stretch ratio), labels (woven/printed, dimensions)

### 3. Measurement Chart (Size Spec)
Garment measurements across all sizes, with tolerance. Format: measurement point name, base size measurement, grading increment per size. Tolerances: ±0.5cm on most measurements; ±1cm on length; ±0.3cm on crotch seam.

**Label every measurement point clearly.** "Chest" is ambiguous — "Chest measured 2.5cm below armhole" is not.

### 4. Stitch Density & Seam Allowance
- Stitch density per inch (SPI): Body seams (12–14 SPI), topstitch (8–10 SPI), overlock
- Seam allowance: Standard 1.0cm on body seams, or specified per seam type
- Seam type: Plain seam, flatlock, French seam, bound seam — specify per seam location

### 5. Colourway Callouts
For each colourway, identify:
- Main fabric colour (Pantone TCX reference)
- Contrast fabric, trims, thread colours
- Print/embroidery colour references (per colour, not just "as shown in sketch")

### 6. Label & Placement Diagrams
For every label (brand label, size label, care label, CPSIA tracking label):
- Position (distance from seam, centre, neck)
- Attachment method (sewn, heat transfer, fold orientation)
- Content requirements (especially for CPSIA and EU care labelling)

### 7. Print / Embroidery Artwork Files
Separate from the flat sketch. Supply vector artwork, at production scale, with colour separations clearly marked. For embroidery: specify stitch type, density, direction.

### 8. Construction Details / Callouts
Annotate the flat sketch with specific instructions for non-obvious construction details: "Flatlock seam on side body — no seam ridge on inside", "Bar-tack at pocket corners — 3 bar tacks minimum", "Elastic casing — casing must NOT enclose elastic ends (turn and stitch at each side seam)."

## What to Skip

- Size range in the tech pack (confirm separately in the purchase order)
- Retail price or cost target (separate conversation)
- Inspiration imagery (save it for your design brief, not the tech pack)
- "As per standard" without defining what standard means (your factory's standard and yours may differ)
